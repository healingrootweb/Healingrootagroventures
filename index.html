Improved Cassava Stems

(TME 419 – IITA Improved)

Early Maturity

✔ TME 419 is an improved cassava variety developed by the International Institute of Tropical Agriculture (IITA) to help farmers achieve faster harvest cycles. Unlike local varieties that may take longer to mature, TME 419 reaches harvestable stage earlier, allowing farmers to plan production efficiently and enjoy quicker returns on investment.

High and Consistent Yield

✔ One of the strongest advantages is its ability to produce high and consistent yields. The variety develops large, well-filled tubers with excellent starch content. Even under varying weather conditions, farmers can rely on stable output.

Improved Disease Resistance

✔ Bred to withstand major cassava diseases, particularly Cassava Mosaic Disease (CMD). This results in healthier plants, reduced crop failure, and lower spending on disease control.

Excellent Processing Quality

✔ Highly suitable for garri, fufu, cassava flour, starch, and industrial raw materials. Its high dry matter content makes it attractive to processors and agro-industries.

Oil Palm Seedlings

(Tenera Variety)

High Oil Yield Potential

✔ Tenera seedlings are the most commercially preferred variety due to their exceptionally high oil content. The fruits contain a thin shell and a thick mesocarp, which significantly increases palm oil extraction rates.

Fast and Vigorous Growth

✔ Carefully selected for strong early growth, allowing them to establish quickly after transplanting. Ensures healthy field performance across Nigeria.

Early Fruiting Advantage

✔ Begins fruiting earlier than unimproved varieties. This means farmers can start harvesting fresh fruit bunches sooner, leading to quicker cash flow.

Long-Term Plantation Investment

✔ A long-life crop capable of producing for 25–30 years. It is a long-term agribusiness investment that guarantees sustained income for decades.
